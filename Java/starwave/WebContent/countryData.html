def convert_to_deg(pos):
    direction = {"N":1, "S":-1, "E":1, "W":-1}
    if pos == "nan":
        return np.nan
    pos = pos.split()
    pos_direction = pos.pop()
    pos.extend([0,0])
    return (int(pos[0]) + int(pos[1])/60.0) * direction[pos_direction]

I hold an assumption that the shapes of most countries can be viewed as convex poligons. In other words, the increase of the perimeter of a shape leads to the increase of its area. From the scatter above, imagine you draw a straight line passing the most points. You will find the points lying on or above the line is much more than the points lying below the line. Therefore, it illustrates that area and boundary are postively related, which verifies my assumption.

The diagram above is the pairwise correlation on all the columns which hold numerical values. The lighter the color is, the higher the two variables are positively correlated; the darker the color is, the higher the two variables are negatively correlated; and, if you look to the bar on the right with 0.0 marked, that means any red color like that indicate the two variables are basically not correlated. For illustration convenience, I use numbers to represent the variable names on the x-axis; 0 refers to "Population", 1 refers to "Life Expectancy", respectively. 

As we know, a variable is always purely correated with itself; that is why the cells on the diagonal have correlation coefficient equaling 1.0. Now we can see some interesting stuff. Population has a significantly high positive correlation with Labor Force (No. 11 on the x-axis), which verifies my observation on Question 1(d). And, Life Expectancy has a high negative correaltion with Commercial Prime Lending Rate (No. 13 on the x-axis). Literacy Rate and Renewable Water Resources (No. 9 on the x-axis) are almost not correlated.
