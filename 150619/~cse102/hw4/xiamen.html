Xiamen City

Places of Interest


Gulang Islet

Xiamen, known as Amoy in the 19th century to westerners, has a long history of over 1,500 years. Gulang Islet is a small island in Xiamen City. During the late period of Qing Dynasty (1644-1911), several western nations set up their consulates on this island. Meanwhile, western missionaries brought modern schooling to this place. Hence, starting from the late 19th century, residents in Gulang Islet felt in love with piano. Today, according to a statistic, there is one piano per family for residents in this islet.


A street in Gulang Islet

Hence, Gulang Islet received a nick name Piano Islet. Moreover, since the islet still kept a large number of consulate buildings of different styles of western nations, it has another nick name called Museum of Buildings from Thousands of Nations. This is a great place to visit, particularly for couples.


Xiamen University

Xiamen University, founded in 1921 by Mr. Tan Kah kee, is famous by its beautiful landscape of campus. Mr. Xian Lin used to study here for 3 months. And, Ms. Sydney Zhao graduated from here. If you pay a visit here, you would definitely receive a taste of the combination of traditional Chinese education and the modern one.
