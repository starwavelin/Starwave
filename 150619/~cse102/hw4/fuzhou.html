Fuzhou City

Places of Interest


Three Lanes and Seven Alleys

Three Lanes and Seven Alleys is a series of traditional streets which can be dated to the 4th century. Today, most ancient buildings in this district were constructed in the Ming Dynasty (1368-1644) and Qing Dynasty (1644-1911). In the year 2006, the City Government of Fuzhou spent over 400 million Chinese currency to renew all the buildings in this district. This huge project was finished in 2010. Today, this old series of streets received a renowned title as One of the Top 10 Chinese Old Streets.


Black Tower

Black Tower is a tower built by granite during the period of the Kingdom of Min (909-945). After over a thousand year's air-slaking, the body of the tower turns into black; that is why local people called it Black Tower.

This tower was built under the order of a king of Min in honor of Buddhism. Today, the Buddhist figures and ancient calligraphy inside the tower are valuable for historical studies.


Flower Lane Church

The Flower Lane Church, located in the downtown of Fuzhou, was originally subordinate to Methodist Church of Fuzhou. This church witnessed the historical change of 20th century Fuzhou; and it is a great honor of the friendship between Fuzhou and the U.S. Christian Methodist church.
